Dear Editor,

I am writing to submit the manuscript titled "Benchmarking knowledge tracing methods across five educational datasets: A comparative study of Bayesian, logistic, and deep learning approaches" for consideration as a Research Paper in the Journal of Learning Analytics.

This study provides a systematic benchmark of five knowledge tracing methods -- Bayesian Knowledge Tracing, Performance Factors Analysis, Deep Knowledge Tracing, Self-Attentive Knowledge Tracing, and a generic causal Transformer baseline -- evaluated across five public datasets under a unified protocol. The benchmark uses student-level cross-validation, a common prediction mask to ensure identical evaluation subsets across model families, five complementary metrics, and student-clustered bootstrap confidence intervals.

The findings are directly relevant to the learning analytics community: they offer empirically grounded guidance for model selection, demonstrate that properly implemented classical methods remain competitive, and provide methodological recommendations (common prediction masks, clustered bootstrap intervals) that strengthen evaluation practice in the field.

This manuscript has not been previously published, nor is it under consideration at another journal. All analysis code and processed data are available in a blinded repository for peer review and will be publicly archived upon acceptance.

I confirm that the work is original, that I meet the journal's authorship criteria, and that I approve the final version of this manuscript.

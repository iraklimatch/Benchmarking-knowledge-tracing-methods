Dear Editor,

I am submitting the manuscript "Benchmarking knowledge tracing methods across five educational datasets: A comparative study of Bayesian, logistic, and deep learning approaches" for consideration as a Research Paper in the Journal of Learning Analytics.

This study provides a controlled comparison of five knowledge tracing methods -- two classical (BKT, PFA) and three deep learning (DKT, SAKT, TransformerKT) -- across five public educational datasets under a single evaluation protocol. The benchmark uses student-level cross-validation, a common prediction mask that ensures all models are scored on identical test subsets, five complementary metrics, and student-clustered bootstrap confidence intervals.

The manuscript is relevant to JLA's scope for three reasons. First, knowledge tracing is a core technique in learning analytics systems that support adaptive learning, early warning, and mastery-based progression. Second, the practical question of which model to deploy given accuracy, interpretability, and cost constraints is directly relevant to learning analytics practitioners. Third, the methodological contributions -- the common prediction mask and clustered bootstrap approach -- address evaluation issues that affect reproducibility across the learning analytics and educational data mining communities.

The complete analysis code and processed data are available in a blinded repository for peer review and will be publicly archived with a DOI upon acceptance.

This work has not been previously published and is not under consideration elsewhere.

Thank you for your consideration.

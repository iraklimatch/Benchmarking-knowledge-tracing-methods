Dear Editor,

I am submitting the manuscript titled "Benchmarking Knowledge Tracing Methods Across Five Educational Datasets: A Comparative Study of Bayesian, Logistic, and Deep Learning Approaches" for consideration as a Regular Paper in IEEE Transactions on Learning Technologies.

This study provides a systematic benchmark of five knowledge tracing methods spanning three methodological generations (Bayesian, logistic, and deep learning), evaluated on five public datasets under a unified protocol with student-level cross-validation, a common prediction mask ensuring identical evaluation subsets, and student-clustered bootstrap confidence intervals.

The manuscript is relevant to TLT's scope because knowledge tracing is a core computational technique underlying adaptive learning systems, intelligent tutoring systems, and learning analytics platforms. The benchmark provides empirically grounded guidance for practitioners selecting among KT methods for technology-enhanced learning applications.

The complete analysis code and processed data are publicly available at https://github.com/iraklimatch/Benchmarking-knowledge-tracing-methods.

This work is original, has not been previously published, and is not under consideration at any other journal. The author declares no conflicts of interest.
